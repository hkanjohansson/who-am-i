Introduction

My name is Håkan and I am 30 years old. Currently I am studying on Linnaeus University in Växjö, Sweden. My first three years I studied the Applied Mathematics program, but after I started to get the fundamentals of programming, I lean more and more towards programming and Machine Learning.

It has not always been clear to me what I wanted to do for a living. When I was about six years old, I started playing a mathematics game called "Chefrens Pyramid". Just a few years ago I found out that the game was for grade four to nine, so it was not that strange that it was hard for me. Anyhow I enjoyed playing it and taking small steps forward every now and then. When starting elementary school, the math classes was way to easy and the school did not give me the next grade math, so I stopped enjoying it. It was not until I decided to fix my grades, where I met a math teacher named Thomas, who was able wake my slumbered interest for mathematics again. Then I went on to do a "base year", where I studied the high school courses in mathematics and physics, and the interest for math just went up more.

Before I started studying I was a truck driver for almost three years. Within five years from completely stopped driving, I had a bachelor degree in mathematics in my hands.

Connection to Mathematics

One thing that came to my mind when I tried to explain summation operator to a friend studying calculus, was that it works more or less in the same way as a for loop. You define a starting point, an endpoint and eventually the step length in each iteration. To see whether or not a loop works, think of the concepts of the principle of induction. First you test one iteration and it works, then you assume that it works for all iterations and then it will probably work for one more.
